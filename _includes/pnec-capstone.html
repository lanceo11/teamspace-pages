A digital home for Poway Neighborhood Emergency Corps

A preparedness platform
Poway can actually use

Building on more than 20 years of PNEC connecting Poway neighbors, we brought the website to life as a living platform: one that shows residents today's local risk, answers their preparedness questions, connects every household to its block, and that volunteers can keep current themselves at no hosting cost.

What this means for Poway

For residents

Ready before they ask

Neighbors see today's local wildfire, flood, and heat outlook the moment they arrive, and can get a clear answer to any preparedness question in seconds.

For neighborhoods

Connected to their block

Every household can find its coordinator, nearest station, and evacuation guidance, strengthening the neighbor-to-neighbor network PNEC is built on.

For PNEC

Owned by the corps

Volunteers keep everything current themselves at no hosting cost, and the platform is ready to grow right alongside the mission.

What Poway gets now

Live, on the homepage

Risk Watch

The moment a resident lands on the site they see today's wildfire, flood, and extreme heat risk for Poway in plain language, updated from current conditions. No app, no login, no guesswork.

Run by volunteers, no developer

Edit and publish in minutes

An approved volunteer can update any page from the browser and publish in minutes. Built-in AI turns a plain request into the change, so PNEC is never waiting on a coder to post an alert or fix a date before an event.

Answers, any time

Helper Bot

A friendly assistant answers preparedness questions instantly, like what belongs in a 72 hour kit or how to find an evacuation route, using PNEC's real programs and resources so the guidance is always on message.

Connects every household

Find Your Neighborhood

An interactive map links each Poway household to its block coordinator, nearest fire station, evacuation guidance, and emergency contacts. This is how the corps reaches people fast when minutes matter.
